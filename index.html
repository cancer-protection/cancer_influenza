Why is the Flu So Dangerous for Immunosuppressed Patients?

Chemotherapy seriously affects the immune system, dramatically reducing white blood cells (neutropenia). This means the body cannot effectively fight the flu virus.

ğŸš¨ IMMEDIATE DANGER

According to the CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention): Patients receiving chemotherapy or radiation in the last month are at increased risk of severe complications from the flu that can lead to death.

Possible Complications for Immunosuppressed Patients

Unlike the healthy population, where flu is usually self-limiting, in immunosuppressed patients it can cause:

  • Pneumonia - the main cause of mortality
  • Secondary bacterial infections
  • Sepsis
  • Acute kidney failure
  • Delay or cancellation of chemotherapy, with consequences for the disease progression
